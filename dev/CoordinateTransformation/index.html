  • $\rho$ - rotation about the local $y$-axis, bringing the $x_{1}$-axis into the $x_{1}$-$y$ plane. This angle is computed automatically.
  • $\chi$ - rotation about the $z_{1}$-axis, bringing the $x_{1}$-axis into coincidence with the global $x^{\prime}$-axis. This angle is computed automatically.
  • $\omega$ - rotation about the $x_{2}$-axis, bringing the $y_{2}$-axis into coincidence with the global $y^{\prime}$-axis and the $z_{2}$-axis into coincidence with the global $z^{\prime}$-axis. This angle must be provided by the user.

\[\begin{align*} \{\mathbf{x}^{\prime}\} &= [\mathbf{\gamma}] \{\mathbf{x}\} \\ &= [\mathbf{\gamma}_{\omega}] [\mathbf{\gamma}_{\chi}] [\mathbf{\gamma}_{\rho}] \{\mathbf{x}\} \\ &= \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & +\cos{\omega} & +\sin{\omega} \\ 0 & -\sin{\omega} & +\cos{\omega} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} +\cos{\chi} & +\sin{\chi} & 0 \\ -\sin{\chi} & +\cos{\chi} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} +\cos{\rho} & 0 & +\sin{\rho} \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -\sin{\rho} & 0 & +\cos{\rho} \end{bmatrix} \{\mathbf{x}\} \end{align*}\]

\[[\mathbf{\Gamma}] = \begin{bmatrix} [\mathbf{\gamma}] & [\mathbf{0}] & [\mathbf{0}] & [\mathbf{0}] \\ [\mathbf{0}] & [\mathbf{\gamma}] & [\mathbf{0}] & [\mathbf{0}] \\ [\mathbf{0}] & [\mathbf{0}] & [\mathbf{\gamma}] & [\mathbf{0}] \\ [\mathbf{0}] & [\mathbf{0}] & [\mathbf{0}] & [\mathbf{\gamma}] \end{bmatrix}\]
